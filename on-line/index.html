on line

23 Dec 2015
Achilles Nasios

Nearly ten years ago I started observing the stratosphere through my lens. I gazed at the endless monotony of the landscape. Nothingness urged me to take pictures. Perhaps it was out of boredom.
Thus, I began distinguishing the small changes that started developing through the comparative contrast of the images.
This constant ritual repetition of the same minimal motive liberated the colour and turned it into a dominant, purely aesthetic force.
So realism remains hidden and shall only be revealed to those who decide to travel deeper into the image itself, only to discover marginalised concepts.
