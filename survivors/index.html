survivors

27 Dec 2015
Achilles Nasios

While everything was fading into white, back in the year 2010, what remained was just the tips of the grass. They stood untouched resisting the wrath of the elements, the ice and the wind.
The vigour of their expression in contrast to their humble existence gradually guided me to Schopenhauer.
Being observed through the lens, Survivors became a part of a never ending Collective Art Memory.
Their shapes were aesthetically and philosophically substantial thus revealing their grandeur.

"One of the most difficult but also interesting facets of the aesthetic experience of the sublime is itâ€™s paradoxical complexity. The sublime is , as Schopenhauer emphasises, a mixed emotional experience: it involves both a feeling of being powerfully overwhelmed, humbled, or dislocated and a sense of being exalted, elevated, or elated." (Bart Vandenabeele - The Sublime in Schopenhauer's Philosophy)

"In praise of the insignificant" Schopenhauer wrote:

"A true intellectual in a state of loneliness can entertain himself successfully delving in his own thoughts visions and fantasies while a constant stream of people spectacles festivities and travels cannot dissuade from torturous boredom of someone who is in spiritual decline."

"We can gather then that the dependance from the external environment is less than what we usually think. It is the omnipotent Time that can rightfully make bodily and intellectual merits to subside. Only the moral aspect is time resilient."
